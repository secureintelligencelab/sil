01 — About

A research lab built
around hard problems.

Leading cybersecurity innovation through interdisciplinary research and close collaboration with industry and academia.

The Secure Intelligence Lab is a research group at Crown Institute of Higher Education dedicated to advancing cybersecurity through novel applications of artificial intelligence and machine learning.

Our interdisciplinary team of researchers, research assistants, and industry partners works collaboratively to address the most pressing security challenges of the digital age — developing solutions that protect critical infrastructure, personal data, and emerging technologies.

We work across threat detection, behavioural analytics, secure communication protocols, and adaptive AI-powered security systems that evolve alongside the threats they defend against.

  • 01AI-Powered Cybersecurity — adaptive defence systems
  • 02Threat & Intrusion Detection — IoV, IoMT, networks
  • 03Machine & Deep Learning — explainable models
  • 04Blockchain Technology — privacy-preserving systems
  • 05Behavioural Analytics — keystroke & identity

03 — Projects

Current research
projects.

Innovative projects tackling tomorrow's security challenges across health, mobility, and finance.

Active

Hierarchical IDS for Zero-Day Attack Detection in IoMT

A hierarchical intrusion detection system to detect zero-day attacks in the Internet of Medical Things using advanced machine learning techniques.

Active

Zero-Knowledge Proofs for Pharmaceutical Traceability

A blockchain-supported pharmaceutical traceability and recall platform designed for the Australian Capital Territory (ACT) public health context.

Active

Explainable Hierarchical Model for Lung & Colon Cancer Detection

An explainable hierarchical deep-learning framework that decomposes diagnosis into clinically meaningful stages, emulating the stepwise reasoning of pathologists.

Active

Keystroke Dynamics–Based Authentication

A hybrid machine-learning authentication framework that leverages keystroke dynamics to verify user identity and detect imposters.

Active

Graph-Based Semi-Supervised Detection of Illicit Bitcoin

A framework addressing the labeling challenge while comparing classical ML, ensemble, deep learning, and graph neural network approaches for detecting illicit Bitcoin transactions.

Completed

Scalable Hierarchical Intrusion Detection for IoV

Hierarchical classification to detect cyberattacks from network traffic in the Internet of Vehicles, achieving 99.2% accuracy.
